🔬 GI Organ Functions

🔴 Esophagus

Muscular tube transporting food via peristalsis. LES prevents reflux. Weakness = GERD. Varices from portal HTN. Achalasia = LES fails to relax.

🟠 Stomach

Secretes HCl + pepsin. Churns food to chyme. Acid + H.pylori = ulcers/gastritis. H2 blockers/PPIs reduce acid. Controls gastric emptying.

🟡 Liver

Detoxification, bile production, clotting factors (II,VII,IX,X), albumin synthesis. Failure = jaundice, coagulopathy, encephalopathy from ammonia.

🟢 Gallbladder

Stores/concentrates bile. Releases when fat enters duodenum (CCK trigger). Gallstones → cholecystitis. Murphy's sign on palpation.

🔵 Pancreas

Exocrine: amylase + lipase. Endocrine: insulin/glucagon. Blocked ducts = pancreatitis (autodigestion). NPO is key management. Lipase more specific than amylase.

🟣 Small Intestine

90% of nutrient absorption. Villi maximize surface area. Celiac disease destroys villi → malabsorption. B12 absorbed in ileum. Crohn's often affects terminal ileum.

⚫ Large Intestine

Water/electrolyte reabsorption, stool formation, gut microbiome. IBD, C.diff, diverticulitis, colorectal cancer. Toxic megacolon risk.

❤️ Appendix

Blind pouch at cecum. Obstruction → appendicitis. McBurney's point. Rupture → peritonitis. NEVER give laxatives/enemas if suspected. SURGICAL EMERGENCY.

🔴 Upper GI Disorders

Esophagus · Stomach · Duodenum · Bleeding & Reflux

🚨 Bleeding Clues: Coffee-ground emesis = digested blood = upper GI source (slower bleed). Bright red hematemesis = active upper GI bleed. Melena (black tarry stool) = upper GI bleed. Hematochezia (bright red rectal blood) = lower GI.

🟡 Lower GI Disorders

Colon · Rectum · Appendix · Bowel patterns

⚠️ Stool Clues: Black/tarry = upper GI. Bright red = lower GI. Pencil-thin = cancer concern. Watery + antibiotic history = C.diff. Fatty/floating = malabsorption. Paradoxical diarrhea in constipated patient = fecal impaction.
